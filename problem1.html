Organic mulches are materials used to prevent weed growth and improve soil health.These materials include bark, compost, and other materials that break down into the soil over time.

Mulching in agriculture refers to the practice of covering the soil around plants with a layer of material like straw, wood chips, leaves, or compost. This layer helps to retain moisture, suppress weed growth, regulate soil temperature, and prevent soil erosion. It also enriches the soil as it decomposes, providing nutrients to the plants.

Mulching is an effective technique used to improve soil health, conserve water, and enhance crop productivity in farming.
